What This Architecture Fixes, and What It Doesn't Claim

This replaces the earlier flawed calculation — 6 layers of 6× recursive multiplication previously produced 622.86 million TPS and 27 petabytes/day of storage growth, both physically impossible with any real infrastructure. This architecture instead uses horizontal sharding (200 nodes, each handling its own regional shard) and Layer-2 batching for micro-transactions, producing figures that are large but checkable against real distributed-systems and cryptographic hardware limits.

This does not claim the underlying business assumptions are realistic. The revenue figures flowing through this architecture (digital ad income, supply chain fees) are the same ones flagged elsewhere in v10.3 as requiring implausible market share (14.4× the global ad market, 13.2% of world GDP in facilitation fees). This document fixes the engineering so it wouldn't collapse under its own numbers — it does not independently verify that those numbers reflect an achievable business.

None of this infrastructure is built. As stated in v10.3's own disclaimer: no cities are under construction, no independent audit has been performed, and the GenAI/quantum layers referenced in the surrounding document remain design specifications, not operational systems.
